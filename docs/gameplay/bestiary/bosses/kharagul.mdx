## Origin

Kharagul was not transformed-it was **drawn forth**. When the Anguish Stone's power reached Stonegrave, it resonated with the Titan remains buried there. From the accumulated rage and hunger of the dead Void Titan and Dark Titan, Kharagul coalesced-a primordial entity that embodies the worst aspects of both cosmic beings.

Unlike other generals who have personalities and histories, Kharagul is barely sentient. It is hunger given form, aggression made manifest. It doesn't hate its enemies-it simply consumes them.

<Callout type="warning">
There is no reasoning with Kharagul. There is no appealing to its nature. It doesn't speak, doesn't negotiate, doesn't show mercy or cruelty. There is only survival or death.
</Callout>

## Strategy

### Recommended Party Composition

| Class | Count | Role |
|-------|-------|------|
| **Paladin** | 1-2 | Tank - Add management, corpse positioning |
| **Cleric** | 1-2 | Healer - Resource conservation, <SkillMention skill="divine_shield" link={false}>Divine Shield</SkillMention> |
| **Mage** | 2-3 | DPS - Magic damage, burst for Phase 3 |

### Key Tactics

1. **Avoid Frontal Cone** - Tanks position Kharagul away from group for Crushing Blow
2. **Heal Through Bleeding** - Rend creates sustained damage that requires attention
3. **Overheal for Passive** - Account for -25% healing received aura
4. **Magic DPS Priority** - Physical damage is heavily mitigated

### Common Mistakes

- Standing in Crushing Blow cone
- Ignoring bleed stacks on multiple targets
- Not accounting for reduced healing effectiveness
- Bringing too many physical damage dealers
