Arcania features a real-time action combat system with tab-targeting elements. Combat is designed to be accessible yet strategic, rewarding positioning, timing, and build optimization.

## Combat Philosophy

### Core Principles

1. **Readable Combat** - Players should always understand why they won or lost
2. **Meaningful Choices** - Ability selection and timing matter
3. **Build Diversity** - Multiple viable approaches per class
4. **Scalable Difficulty** - Easy to learn, challenging to master

## Attack System

### Basic Attacks

**Auto-Attack:**
- Automatic attacks when target is selected
- Speed determined by weapon and equipment bonuses

<FormulaBlock title="Attack Speed">
Attacks Per Second = Base Weapon Speed × (1 + Attack Speed Bonus)
Soft Cap: 2.5 APS (50% efficiency beyond)
</FormulaBlock>

## Damage Calculation

All abilities scale from **derived stats** (<DerivedStatMention stat="physical_attack" link={false}>Physical Attack</DerivedStatMention> or <DerivedStatMention stat="magic_attack" link={false}>Magic Attack</DerivedStatMention>), not primary stats directly. This means equipment directly affects ability damage.

### Physical Damage (Warrior, Paladin, Ranger)

<FormulaBlock title="Physical Ability Damage">
Ability Damage = Base Damage + (Physical Attack × Skill Multiplier)
Defense Reduction = Enemy Defense / (Enemy Defense + 100)
Final Damage = Ability Damage × (1 - Defense Reduction)
</FormulaBlock>

**Example:** Warrior's <SkillMention skill="power_strike">Power Strike</SkillMention> (×1.5 multiplier)
- <DerivedStatMention stat="physical_attack" link={false}>Physical Attack</DerivedStatMention>: 200 (from equipment)
- Base Damage: 100
- Ability Damage = 100 + (200 × 1.5) = 400

### Magic Damage (Mage, Cleric)

<FormulaBlock title="Magic Ability Damage">
Ability Damage = Base Damage + (Magic Attack × Skill Multiplier)
Resistance Reduction = Enemy Resistance / (Enemy Resistance + 120)
Final Damage = Ability Damage × (1 - Resistance Reduction)
</FormulaBlock>

**Example:** Mage's <SkillMention skill="fireball">Fireball</SkillMention> (×1.5 multiplier)
- <DerivedStatMention stat="magic_attack" link={false}>Magic Attack</DerivedStatMention>: 250 (from equipment)
- Base Damage: 120
- Ability Damage = 120 + (250 × 1.5) = 495

<Callout type="info">
Equipment with +<DerivedStatMention stat="physical_attack" link={false}>Physical Attack</DerivedStatMention> or +<DerivedStatMention stat="magic_attack" link={false}>Magic Attack</DerivedStatMention> directly increases ability damage. A weapon with +50 Physical Attack adds +50 × skill multiplier to every physical ability!
</Callout>

### <DerivedStatMention stat="critical_chance" link={false}>Critical Hits</DerivedStatMention>

<FormulaBlock title="Critical Hit Formula">
Critical Chance = 5% + Equipment Bonus
Critical Chance Cap: 50%

Critical Damage = 125% + Equipment Bonus
Critical Damage Cap: 200%
</FormulaBlock>

<Callout type="info">
All characters have a base 5% critical chance. Additional critical chance and damage come from equipment. Agility-focused gear often provides crit bonuses.
</Callout>

## Defense System

### Miss Chance

Attacks have a chance to miss based on the level difference between attacker and target. Higher level targets are harder to hit, and this applies to both players and monsters.

<FormulaBlock title="Miss Chance">
Miss Chance = 5% + (Level Difference) × 2%
Cap: 40%
</FormulaBlock>

| Scenario | Miss Chance |
|----------|-------------|
| Same level | 5% |
| Target +5 levels | 15% |
| Target +10 levels | 25% |
| Target +15 levels | 35% |
| Target +18+ levels | 40% (cap) |

<Callout type="info">
This system works both ways: your attacks miss more against higher level enemies, and lower level monsters miss more against you. Outleveling an area provides natural survivability as enemies struggle to hit you.
</Callout>

### Penetration

Penetration allows attackers to bypass a portion of enemy defenses.

<FormulaBlock title="Armor Penetration">
<DerivedStatMention stat="armor_penetration" link={false}>Armor Penetration</DerivedStatMention> = Equipment Bonus
Cap: 40%

Effective Enemy Armor = Enemy Armor × (1 - Armor Penetration)
</FormulaBlock>

<FormulaBlock title="Magic Penetration">
<DerivedStatMention stat="magic_penetration" link={false}>Magic Penetration</DerivedStatMention> = Equipment Bonus
Cap: 40%

Effective Enemy Resistance = Enemy Magic Resistance × (1 - Magic Penetration)
</FormulaBlock>

<Callout type="warning">
Penetration comes entirely from equipment. Against heavily armored targets, investing in penetration can be more effective than raw damage stats.
</Callout>

### Diminishing Returns

<Callout type="info">
Crowd control abilities have diminishing returns to prevent stun-lock situations.
</Callout>

- **First CC:** Full duration
- **Second CC (within 15s):** 50% duration
- **Third CC (within 15s):** 25% duration
- **Fourth CC (within 15s):** Immune for 15s

**Boss Immunity:**
- Boss monsters are immune to hard CC (stun, freeze)
- Bosses can be slowed/weakened at reduced effect

## Death & Resurrection

### Death Penalties

Death penalties vary by zone type:

| Zone Type | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| 🟡 PvE Zone | **40% durability loss** on all equipped items |
| 🟠 Hard PvE Zone | **80% durability loss** on all equipped items |
| 🔴 PvP Zone | **Random items drop** from Bag (equipped items safe) |

<Callout type="warning">
**Item Destruction:** If an item reaches 0% durability, it is **permanently destroyed**. Always repair your gear at a Blacksmith NPC before it breaks!
</Callout>

Additional effects on death:
- Respawn at nearest Safe Zone or outpost
- 10-second Resurrection Sickness (reduced stats)

For complete death mechanics, see [Death Penalties](/docs/platform/zones/death-penalties).

## Balance Targets

### Time-to-Kill (TTK)

| Scenario | Target TTK |
|----------|------------|
| Player vs Hostile Monster (same level) | 5-8 seconds |
| Player vs Elite Monster (same level) | 15-25 seconds |
| Full Party vs Boss | 2-5 minutes |

### Healing vs Damage

- Healers should not outheal focused damage from 2+ DPS
- Solo healer should sustain party against reasonable damage
- Self-healing should not make classes unkillable
