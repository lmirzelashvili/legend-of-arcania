## Homeland Quests (Levels 1-15)

Every player begins in their racial homeland, learning the traditions and skills of their people.

### Meridia (Humans)

| Quest Chain | Focus | Reward |
|-------------|-------|--------|
| **The Orphan's Path** | Learn Steel Covenant basics | Starting weapon |
| **Defender's Training** | Combat fundamentals | Armor set |
| **The King's Road** | Introduction to the war | Travel pass to Valoryn |

**Narrative:** You are an orphan of the Duskfall era, raised in Meridia's academies. Your training prepares you for the journey every human warrior makes-to Valoryn, where the real war begins.

### Lythora (Lilin)

| Quest Chain | Focus | Reward |
|-------------|-------|--------|
| **Whispers in the Dark** | Shadow and stealth basics | Racial ability unlock |
| **The Balance Path** | Understanding void philosophy | Spiritual focus item |
| **Northern Journey** | Preparing for the long march | Forest survival kit |

**Narrative:** The Whisper Glades have chosen you. Empress Lilith herself has marked you for the war effort. You must prove worthy of leaving Lythora's protection.

### Redmire (Darkan)

| Quest Chain | Focus | Reward |
|-------------|-------|--------|
| **Forge Trial** | Combat basics and proving worth | Volcanic steel ingot |
| **The Hunger's Test** | Resisting the Dark Titan's echo | Spiritual seal |
| **Descent from Mountains** | Preparing for surface war | Heavy travel gear |

**Narrative:** Every Darkan must face the Hunger before leaving Redmire-proving they can control the Dark Titan's drive. Only then may you descend to join the Alliance.

### Lumeria (Luminar)

| Quest Chain | Focus | Reward |
|-------------|-------|--------|
| **Light's Awakening** | Channeling divine essence | Focus crystal |
| **The Vigil's Duty** | Understanding your mother's legacy | Flame blessing |
| **The Western Path** | Preparing for mortal realms | Ley-travel token |

**Narrative:** You have felt Anias's light since birth. Now the Seraph Council has deemed you ready to carry that light into the darkness. The war needs Luminar more than ever.

## Journey to Valoryn (Levels 15-20)

After completing homeland training, every player makes the journey to Valoryn. This quest chain varies by race but converges at the central bastion.

### Shared Experiences

Regardless of origin, all players experience:
- **First Contact** - Meeting members of other races
- **Refugee Escort** - Protecting civilians fleeing the Dominion
- **Enemy Sighting** - First encounter with Anguish creatures
- **Valoryn Gates** - Arrival at the bastion

<Callout type="info">
The Journey to Valoryn is designed to introduce cross-racial cooperation. You'll group with players from other homelands, building bonds that will matter in the Dominion campaign.
</Callout>

## Alliance Training (Levels 20-30)

At Valoryn, you join the Grand Alliance formally. Training quests prepare you for the Dominion campaign.

### Core Training Chains

| Quest Chain | Focus | Reward |
|-------------|-------|--------|
| **War Council Briefing** | Learn the five Dominions | Campaign map |
| **Cross-Training** | Work with other classes | Team tactics |
| **Equipment Requisition** | Prepare for war | T2 gear set |
| **First Patrol** | Dominion border skirmishes | Combat experience |

### Proving Grounds

Before entering the Dominions, players must complete **Proving Grounds** challenges:

| Challenge | Test | Reward |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **Solo Trial** | Individual combat skill | Personal commendation |
| **Party Trial** | Group coordination | Party commendation |
| **Survival Trial** | Resource management | Field supplies |
| **Lore Trial** | Knowledge of the enemy | Intelligence dossier |

## Dominion Campaign (Levels 30-55)

The heart of Arcania's quest structure: a progressive campaign through five Dominion zones, each culminating in a boss encounter.

### Campaign Progression

```
Valoryn → Dreadmar Frontier → Mire of Whispers → Stonegrave Expanse → Eclipse Barrens → Hall of Fallen → Throne of Ruin
```

## Legendary Quests (Level 55+)

Endgame quests that provide T5 equipment, unique abilities, and story conclusions.

### Quest Categories

| Category | Focus | Reward Type |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| **Weapon Legends** | Class-specific weapon quests | T5 Legendary weapons |
| **Armor Legends** | Set completion quests | T5 armor sets |
| **Lore Secrets** | Hidden story content | Unique cosmetics, titles |
| **The Final War** | Throne of Ruin assault | Story conclusion |

### The Throne of Ruin

The ultimate quest: confronting Alassa herself.

<Callout type="warning" title="Multiple Endings">
The Throne of Ruin quest has multiple possible endings based on player choices throughout the campaign. Options include:
- **Destroy Alassa** - End the war through force
- **Redeem Alassa** - Attempt to reach the goddess within
- **Sacrifice** - A harder path with greater cost
- **The Titan's Return** - If certain conditions are met (or failed)

Your choices throughout the Dominion campaign affect which endings are available.
</Callout>

## Quest Types

### Main Story Quests
- Required for progression
- Unlock new zones and content
- Provide major lore revelations

### Side Quests
- Optional but rewarding
- Often provide unique items or abilities
- Flesh out world and characters

### Daily Quests
- Repeatable for resources
- Vary by zone and level
- Provide steady progression

### Weekly Quests
- Larger objectives
- Better rewards
- Often require groups

### World Events
- Server-wide objectives
- Limited-time content
- Unique rewards

## Party Quests

Many Dominion quests require or strongly encourage grouping:

| Quest Type | Recommended Party | Notes |
|------------|------------------|-------|
| **Story Main** | Solo or duo | Scaled difficulty |
| **Zone Elite** | 3-person party | Standard group content |
| **Boss Preparation** | 5-person party | Pre-boss chains |
| **Boss Encounter** | 5-person party | Required for generals |
| **Legendary** | 5-10 players | Endgame challenges |

<Callout type="tip">
The Dominion campaign is designed for parties of mixed races and classes. Cross-racial cooperation isn't just thematic-it's mechanically optimal.
</Callout>
