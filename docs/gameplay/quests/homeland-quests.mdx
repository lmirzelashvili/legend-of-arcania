### Story Summary

Captain Marcus Steele finds you among the refugees fleeing the encroaching corruption. Unlike most survivors, you show potential. He takes you under his wing, teaching the basics of the Steel Covenant philosophy: strength through discipline, purpose through service.

Your journey takes you through the **Golden Fields** where wolves have turned rabid from the corruption, along **Pilgrim's Road** where bandits prey on desperate refugees, and into the **Briarwood Depths** where the forest itself has become hostile.

At **Watchfire Ridge**, you witness the fall of the last human outpost before Valoryn. The Watchfire Betrayer - once a trusted commander - has turned to the Dominion. Defeating him proves you're ready for the true war.

> "You came to me a refugee. You leave a soldier. Make Meridia proud."
> - <img src="/game-icons/avatars/human_npc/marcus_steele_avatar.png" alt="" className="inline w-5 h-5 mr-1 my-0 rounded-full align-middle" /> Captain Marcus Steele

### Story Summary

Elder Thessaly has watched over the First Forest for centuries. Now she watches it die. The Anguish Stone's corruption has turned the forest's guardians into nightmares, and dreams themselves have become weapons.

You journey through **Dreamveil Hollow** where the last pure dreams persist, into **Whisperwood Descent** where those dreams turn to nightmares. The **Thornveil Passage** shows you how far the corruption has spread - even the protective thorns that once shielded the forest now attack all living things.

At **Rootwarden's Grave**, you face the ultimate tragedy: the Rootwarden who protected Lythora for millennia has become a creature of pure wrath. Thessaly weeps as you put the ancient guardian down.

> "The forest remembers what it was. Perhaps one day, we can help it remember again. But first, we must survive."
> - <img src="/game-icons/avatars/lilin_npc/thessaly_avatar.png" alt="" className="inline w-5 h-5 mr-1 my-0 rounded-full align-middle" /> Elder Thessaly

### Story Summary

Forgemaster Grom doesn't believe in gentle training. The volcanic highlands of Redmire are unforgiving, and so is he. But beneath his harsh exterior is a teacher who believes every Darkan can overcome the stigma of their Dark Titan heritage.

Your journey begins in the **Ember Peaks** where the volcanic heat is constant and the creatures are adapted to fire. You descend through **Ironblood Descent**, the ancient mining tunnels where Darkan forged their greatest weapons. The **Ashfall Barrens** test your endurance in a scorched wasteland.

At the **Smoldering Gates**, you face the Gatekeeper of Ruin - a construct that once protected Redmire but has been corrupted by the Anguish Stone. Defeating it proves what Grom always believed: Darkan are masters of fire, not its servants.

> "The Dark Titan's blood runs in your veins. So does the strength to resist it. You choose what defines you."

### Story Summary

Lightkeeper Seris struggles with a question that torments all Luminar: if they carry the light of Anias, why is it fading? The **Shattered Spires** that once blazed with divine radiance now stand dark and broken. Something is consuming the light itself.

Your journey takes you through **Fading Light Trail** where the last glimmers of divine power persist, into **Duskfall Passage** where shadows overtake everything. At **Twilight's Edge**, you stand at the boundary between light and absolute darkness.

The boss **Twilight's End** is a philosophical enemy - an entity that represents the death of faith itself. Defeating it teaches Seris a crucial lesson: the Eternal Light doesn't come from Anias. It comes from those who carry it. The goddess may be dead, but her light lives on in mortal hearts.
