Gems are crystallized power sources that socket into equipment. Each gem randomly rolls ONE stat from its category, creating exciting double-RNG for god roll hunting.

---

## How Gems Work

1. **Get a gem** (<GemMention gem="anguish" link={false}>Anguish</GemMention>, <GemMention gem="void" link={false}>Void</GemMention>, or <GemMention gem="light" link={false}>Light</GemMention>)
2. **Stat rolls randomly** from that gem's pool
3. **Value rolls** within ±20% range
4. **Socket it** into any equipment with an empty socket

<Callout type="info">
Two layers of RNG: which stat you get AND how good that stat rolls.
</Callout>

## Socketing Mechanics

- **Socketing:** Insert gems into any empty socket
- **Permanent:** Once socketed, gems cannot be removed normally
- **Removal:** Only a <MaterialMention material="hand_of_blacksmith">Hand of Blacksmith</MaterialMention> can remove a socketed gem
- **On Removal:** The gem is destroyed, but the item remains intact

<Callout type="warning">
Gems are permanent once socketed. Using a <MaterialMention material="hand_of_blacksmith" link={false}>**Hand of Blacksmith**</MaterialMention> removes the gem (destroying it) to free the socket for a new gem.
</Callout>

## Removing Gems

Once socketed, gems cannot be removed by normal means. The only way to free a socket is using a <MaterialMention material="hand_of_blacksmith" link={false}>**Hand of Blacksmith**</MaterialMention>.

| Action | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Use <MaterialMention material="hand_of_blacksmith" link={false}>Hand of Blacksmith</MaterialMention> | Socket becomes empty |
| Gem | **Destroyed** |
| Equipment | Remains intact |

This allows you to replace a bad gem roll with a better one. The <MaterialMention material="hand_of_blacksmith" link={false}>Hand of Blacksmith</MaterialMention> is consumed in the process.
