### Origin
Born amidst the wreckage of **The Anguish**-the catastrophic clash between the Void Titan and Dark Titan-Marael emerged as the first conscious being in existence. For millennia, he wandered alone, bringing structure to the chaos left by his creators' destruction.

### Character
Marael is **order personified**. He values structure, predictability, and balance above all else. His love for Anias was genuine, but he always feared her compassion would lead to ruin. He was right-but not in the way he expected.

### The Journey
1. **Cosmic Wanderer** - Shaped the laws of reality in solitude
2. **Discoverer of Anias** - Found love and purpose at last
3. **Father and Partner** - Built Arcania with Anias, raised Erion and Alassa
4. **Grieving Avenger** - Turned to vengeance after Anias's murder
5. **Reluctant Ally** - Joined Erion after witnessing the Dark Titan's influence on Alassa

<Callout type="info">
Marael's defection to the resistance was not born of mercy but of fear. When he saw the Dark Titan's will reaching through Alassa, he realized the war had escaped his control. The enemy was no longer mortal betrayal-it was cosmic resurgence.
</Callout>

### Origin
Anias was born from the **Echoes of Regret**-the sorrow and guilt of the Titans' annihilation. While she slumbered, her unconscious mind radiated creative energy, forming the **First World**. When Marael found and awakened her, she became the first being to choose creation over chaos.

### Character
Anias embodied **unconditional compassion**. Where Marael saw mortal flaws, she saw potential. Her belief in mortals drove her to create Arcania-a land where gods and mortals could coexist. This compassion was her greatest strength and her fatal weakness.

### Death
On the eve of Erion's wedding, **Nimue**-his mortal bride-murdered Anias with the **Arcblade**, believing it would grant her immortality. Anias, knowing Nimue's intent, chose not to defend herself. Her final act was giving Erion the **Spark of Light** and asking him to protect her dream.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Eternal Light">
Anias's death did not destroy her essence-it distributed it. Her life-giving energy seeped into all living things, becoming the **Eternal Light** that Clerics channel. She is gone, but she is everywhere.
</Callout>

### Origin
Erion was the firstborn son of Marael and Anias, inheriting his father's strength and his mother's compassion. For ages, he existed in divine perfection-powerful but untested, immortal but unliving. Only when gods descended to live among mortals did he discover **love**.

### Character
Erion is **conflicted nobility**. He carries the weight of his mother's death, his father's wrath, his sister's corruption, and his wife's betrayal. Despite this burden, he has not become bitter. He fights not for vengeance but for hope-the belief that his mother's dream can still be realized.

### The Spark of Light
When Anias died, she gave Erion the **Spark of Light**-the first light she ever created, the illumination she brought to Marael's darkness. This essence is the foundation of Paladin power. Those mortals who carry fragments of the Spark become living shields against the darkness.

<Callout type="tip">
Erion is the source of all Paladin power. When players choose the Paladin class, they are receiving a fragment of the Spark that Anias gave her son-carrying a piece of divine light into battle.
</Callout>

### Origin
Alassa was the luminous daughter of Marael and Anias-a goddess of beauty and grace who sought connection with mortals. Unlike her brother, who found lasting love, Alassa found only betrayal.

### The Fall
When Alassa discovered that **Cassian**, the mortal general she loved, had betrayed her with a servant, her heartbreak became rage. She killed the servant and her family, then cursed Cassian to wander as a hollow shell. Anias, devastated by her daughter's actions, banished Alassa to the Eterna Citadel for a hundred years.

In that isolation, heartbreak and betrayal carved deep scars. Her compassionate heart grew cold. When Anias was murdered and Marael called for vengeance, Alassa was ready to become his weapon.

### <AnguishStoneMention link={false}>The Anguish Stone</AnguishStoneMention>
Marael gave Alassa the **<AnguishStoneMention>Anguish Stone</AnguishStoneMention>**-an artifact forged from the essence of the Titans' clash. With it, she commands armies of nightmare creatures and corrupts everything she touches. But the Stone also connects her to the **Dark Titan's will**, which grows stronger with each passing year.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Question of Agency">
It is unclear how much of Alassa's actions are her own choice. The Anguish Stone's influence grows constantly. Some believe she is a victim as much as a villain-that the goddess who once loved mortals is still trapped somewhere inside the Dread Emissary.

Others believe she died long ago, and only the Stone remains, wearing her face.
</Callout>

## Family Dynamics

The divine family's relationships form the emotional core of Arcania's war:

| Relationship | Nature | Current Status |
|--------------|--------|----------------|
| **Marael & Anias** | Deep love, philosophical conflict | Severed by death |
| **Marael & Erion** | Father and son, now allies | Reconciled through necessity |
| **Marael & Alassa** | Father and weapon | Broken when he saw the Titan's influence |
| **Erion & Alassa** | Siblings, now enemies | He still hopes to save her |
| **Anias & Her Children** | Unconditional love | Her death drives everything |

<Callout type="info">
The final confrontation between Erion and Alassa will not simply be a battle. It will be a reckoning between siblings-one who inherited their mother's hope, and one who inherited their father's wrath (before even he turned away).
</Callout>
