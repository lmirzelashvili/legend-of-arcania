### Military Structure

The Alliance operates through a unified command:

```
Erion (Supreme Command)
    ├── Valoryn Defense Force (permanent garrison)
    ├── Dominion Campaign Forces (offensive operations)
    │   ├── Dreadmar Division
    │   ├── Mire Division
    │   ├── Stonegrave Division
    │   ├── Eclipse Division
    │   └── Hall Assault Force
    └── Homeland Defense Forces (racial militias)
```

### Alliance Principles

1. **Unity Over Origin** - Race does not determine worth; only actions matter
2. **Shared Sacrifice** - Every homeland contributes; no race bears burden alone
3. **Hope Over Vengeance** - Fight to save, not merely to destroy
4. **Honor the Fallen** - Remember those who died so others might live

The Restorationists are a secret faction within the Alliance who believe Anias can be truly restored-not just remembered, but resurrected. They seek to gather enough of her scattered essence to reform her divine body.

### Beliefs

- The Eternal Light is Anias's consciousness, distributed but intact
- Lumeria's growing crystals prove she is slowly reforming
- With enough faith and gathered essence, she could return
- Her return would end the war instantly

### Controversy

<Callout type="warning" title="Heresy or Hope?">
The orthodox Luminar church considers Restorationism heresy. They argue:
- Anias chose her fate; mortals should not interfere
- Resurrection might bring back something changed, corrupted
- The focus should be on honoring her sacrifice, not undoing it

The Restorationists counter:
- A mother would want to return to her children
- The war proves her sacrifice wasn't enough
- Only she can truly defeat Marael's darkness

The debate threatens to fracture Luminar unity at a critical time.
</Callout>

The Hollow are Paladins whose Spark was extinguished-warriors who broke their oath or succumbed to hatred. They wander Arcania as empty husks, neither alive nor dead, neither allied nor hostile.

### Nature

- **Not Undead** - They still breathe, eat, exist
- **Not Living** - They feel nothing, want nothing, hope for nothing
- **Not Hostile** - They attack only if threatened
- **Not Allied** - They cannot be recruited or reasoned with

### Origin

A Paladin becomes Hollow when:
- They break their sacred oath
- They succumb to hatred or vengeance
- They lose faith in Erion's cause
- Their Spark is forcibly extracted (rare, requires powerful magic)

<Callout type="info">
Clerics discovered they can potentially restore Hollow Paladins by reigniting the Eternal Light within them. This controversial practice has saved some, but others argue it violates natural law-that becoming Hollow is a consequence that should not be reversed.
</Callout>

## Minor Factions

### The Dream Weavers
Elite Lilin mages who specialize in nature magic and Dream Walking. They serve Empress Lilith directly and handle the Alliance's most delicate intelligence and magical operations.

### The Forge Guilds
Darkan organizations that produce the Alliance's weapons and armor. Each guild jealously guards its techniques and competes for prestige.

### The Steel Covenant
Not a faction per se, but a philosophy with organized adherents. Human warriors who reject magical dependency and train purely in mortal combat arts.

### The Dreamguard Knights
Lilin who became Paladins, blending the Spark of Light with their ancient dream magic. They serve as bridges between the Luminar and Lilin, embodying both divine radiance and the First World's primal power.

### The Dreamwalkers
Luminar Rangers who successfully connected to the First World despite their conscious-light nature. They explore the boundary between divine creation and primal dream.
